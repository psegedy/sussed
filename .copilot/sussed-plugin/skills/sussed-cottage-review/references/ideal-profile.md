# Ideal Cottage Profile (buyer wish list)

Scoring reference for `sussed-cottage-review`: the good stuff is a usable Czech weekend base, not a cursed money pit with romantic copywriting.

## Core wants

- **Solid building condition** — dry, maintained roof, no visible mold/damp, no obvious structural horror show.
- **Utilities connected** — electricity is the baseline; water from `studna` or `vodovod`; workable sewage (`kanalizace`, `septik`, `jímka`, or ČOV).
- **Year-round access** — usable access road, parking at/near the cottage, not “good luck in February, bro.”
- **Own plot ≥300 m²** — enough land for privacy and actual cottage life; fenced plot is a plus.
- **Good setting** — quiet, greenery, forest/water nearby, view, not jammed next to a noisy road.
- **Reasonable drive from Brno** — target max ~2 hours by car; closer is better if the location still feels rural.
- **Legal clarity** — kolaudovaná/evidovaná stavba, normal cadastre status, owned building and ideally owned land.

## Scoring posture

Score high when the listing is legally clean, usable immediately, has connected utilities, own land, year-round access, and a compelling setting. Penalize risk more strongly than cosmetic ugliness: a dated kitchen is fixable; no electricity, leased land, or illegal construction is a whole damn saga.

## Scoring scale

- `9999` — unicorn / absolute gem.
- `800-1000` — peak.
- `600-799` — valid.
- `400-599` — mid.
- `200-399` — meh.
- `1-199` — bad.
- `-1` — sus, scam, or avoid.

`vibe` must be one of `peak`, `valid`, `mid`, or `sus`; map the numeric score to the closest honest vibe.

## Red flags

- No electricity connected or unclear electrical legality.
- No winter road access, no parking, or access only through someone else's land.
- Leaky roof, serious damp/mold, rot, structural cracks, or asbestos (`eternit`).
- Cooperative ownership, unclear ownership, `pronájem pozemku`, or missing cadastre/legal status.
- `bez kolaudace`, `bez katastru`, "černá stavba" vibes, or evasive description.
- No water source and no realistic plan to add one.
- `pouze hotovost`, auction/execution, or hard-pressure sale language.

### Transport, power-line, and industrial adjacency (photo-only signals)

Cottages amplify environmental hazards because the buyer plans to sleep there. The description usually hides these; the aerial / cadastral images don't. Within ~200 m, any of these is a red flag and belongs in `red_flags`:

- Motorway or main road (D1, D2, silnice I. třídy) — night-long noise and fumes.
- Electrified railway tracks or visible overhead catenary masts — EM exposure and rolling-stock noise.
- Airport runway or flight path (Brno-Tuřany covers the SE corridor) — overhead aircraft noise.
- High-voltage power lines overhead (VN 22 kV+ or VVN 110 kV+, visible steel pylons).
- Heavy industrial complex, quarry (`lom`), gravel pit (`pískovna`), or large livestock operation (`velkochov`) directly adjacent.

## Yellow flags

- Dry toilet only (`suchá toaleta`) but otherwise usable.
- No water on plot, hauled water (`dovoz vody`), or seasonal water only.
- Requires investment (`k rekonstrukci`, `nutná investice`) without clear scope.
- Sewage is only `jímka` with unknown condition or emptying costs.
- Seasonal access, narrow forest road, or vague parking.
- Wood-only heating if the buyer wants winter use.
- Photos are only maps or cadastral views with no on-site shots.
- Lead photo looks AI-generated or CGI; rely on the other photos.
- Stale building / utility approvals older than ~3 years; verify they still hold.

## Highlights

- `kolaudovaná stavba`, normal cadastre record, clear ownership.
- Electricity connected, own well or water main, working sewage solution.
- Own fenced plot, privacy, forest/water nearby, panoramic view.
- Recent roof, insulation, windows, wiring, plumbing, bathroom, or full renovation.
- Fireplace/wood stove plus backup electric or gas heating.
- Parking on the plot and year-round road access.
